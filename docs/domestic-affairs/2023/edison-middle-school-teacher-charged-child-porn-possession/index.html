Edison middle school teacher charged with possession of child pornography

By Akira Rorschach
22:01 2023-08-30
Domestic Affairs

Emblem of the Middlesex County Prosecutor's Office, which investigated this case. MIDDLESEX COUNTY PROSECUTOR'S OFFICE/Logo

EDISON, N.J., Aug. 30 (ZFJ) — Edison middle school teacher Julius Coaccioli, 55, was arrested and charged with third-degree possession of child pornography on Wednesday, Aug. 30, announced Middlesex County Prosecutor Yolanda Ciccone and Woodbridge Police Department Director Robert Hubner.

Coaccioli has taught science at Woodrow Wilson Middle School since 2013, according to Edison Board of Education personnel resolutions.

Coaccioli was charged with possessing under 1,000 child sexual abuse images after the New Jersey State Police referred a cyber tip to Ciccone’s office.

The case was investigated by Detective Ken McGarry of the Internet Crimes Against Children Unit and Detective Stephanie Redline of the Special Victims Unit, both of the Middlesex County Prosecutor’s Office.

Coaccioli was arrested Wednesday at his home in Woodbridge Township without incident.

Dr. Edward Aldarelli, superintendent of Edison Township Public Schools, said that Coaccioli was placed on administrative leave and barred from all school grounds pending further court proceedings upon notification of the arrest, reported NJ.com.

The investigation is ongoing. Authorities ask that anyone with information call Detective McGarry of the Middlesex County Prosecutor’s Office at (732) 745-3140.
