Pedestrian fatally struck by car in Gaithersburg

By Alvin Wu
17:32 2024-10-05
Domestic Affairs

A photo of Dr. Dominique Mwepu standing in front of the United States Capitol in Washington, D.C. GOFUNDME/Handout

COLLEGE PARK, Md., Oct. 5 (ZFJ) ‚Äî Dr. Dominique Mwepu, 53, was fatally struck by a car on Monday, Sept. 30, according to the Montgomery County Police Department.

At about 7:55 p.m., county police officers from the 5th district and fire rescue personnel responded to reports of a collision involving a blue Honda Prelude and a pedestrian at the intersection of Frederick Road and High Point Drive.

Mwepu sustained life-threatening injuries and was pronounced dead on the scene.

Mwepu was a lecturer for the Department of Hearing and Speech Sciences at the University of Maryland, College Park.

A GoFundMe fundraiser set up to support his family says that Mwepu had been returning after dropping off a U-Haul truck used to move his family to a new home when he had been struck. Mwepu leaves behind his wife Mireille and son Joshua.

A memorial service will be held for Mwepu at the UMD Garden Chapel on Wednesday, Oct. 9, from 12 p.m. to 2 p.m., to ‚Äúhonor and remember his remarkable life and spirit,‚Äù according to an email sent to HESP undergraduate students.
